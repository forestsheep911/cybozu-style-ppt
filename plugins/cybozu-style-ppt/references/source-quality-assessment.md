# Source Quality Assessment

## Assessment

The downloaded PPTX corpus is useful as a brand-signal corpus, not as a design-quality benchmark.

Many source decks are old, rough, dense, or mechanically assembled. Even the newer 2025-2026 decks are mostly consistent rather than well designed. They provide a recognizable kintone/cybozu house style, but they should not set the quality bar for generated decks.

## Keep

Use these source-derived elements as stable brand cues:

- kintone yellow as the primary accent;
- white or pale cream backgrounds;
- yellow top/bottom rules on proposal-style slides;
- small kintone logo placement near slide corners;
- yellow cloud badge around the kintone mark;
- black/dark navy bold headings;
- light gray dividers and simple bordered panels;
- screenshot-plus-callout product explanation pattern;
- app-pack maps and simple workflow/process diagrams;
- bright, friendly, low-formality tone.

## Do Not Imitate

Do not imitate these common weaknesses from the corpus:

- crowded slides with too many screenshots;
- tiny unreadable tables;
- weak hierarchy where title, body, and labels compete;
- inconsistent icon systems;
- rough clip-art or generic PowerPoint figures;
- customer logo walls without editorial structure;
- old cyan gradient template backgrounds unless explicitly requested;
- tables used as the primary visual on presentation slides;
- visual density that only works as a document, not as a presentation.

## Character Assets

The small people and mascot-like figures in the corpus are not a strong differentiator. Many appear to be generic clip-art, stock PowerPoint-like illustrations, screenshots, or raster assets embedded in the decks.

For this skill:

- Do not treat source-deck small people as a mandatory cybozu style.
- Use people/mascot assets only when they clarify the business scenario.
- Prefer a small, deliberately redrawn character set if character assets become important.
- Avoid copying extracted bitmap people into reusable assets unless usage is approved.

## Updated Direction

The skill should not aim to reproduce the average internal cybozu deck. It should produce a better editable PPTX that borrows only the recognizable brand cues.

Design target:

- cleaner hierarchy than the source decks;
- fewer objects per slide;
- stronger claim titles;
- more deliberate component grammar;
- controlled use of yellow;
- editable native elements;
- product screenshots only where they provide evidence;
- no full-slide bitmap output unless Route B is explicitly requested.

In short: use the corpus for identity, not taste.
