# Text Lock: kintone Field Feedback Loop

Audience: business users and managers evaluating whether kintone can reduce scattered field reporting and turn it into repeated improvement.

Language for this test: English. The asset catalog should remain language-neutral; copy can later be localized to Japanese or Chinese.

## 00_cover

- **Claim**: Turn scattered field feedback into one visible improvement loop.
- **Support**: Paper, spreadsheet, and chat-based updates are hard to compare. kintone can collect field input, approval status, notifications, and improvement actions in one shared work system.
- **Speaker note**: This opens the story with a concrete operational loop rather than a feature list.
- **Must keep**: `field feedback`, `improvement loop`, `kintone`.
- **Free to change**: Scene, character expression, background rhythm, and supporting visual metaphor.
- **Route guidance**: Route A should use editable title/text and existing assets. Route B can create a more emotional scene from the same claim.

## 01_pain

- **Claim**: When updates live in separate places, the team loses the pattern.
- **Support**: A worker reports an issue, a manager checks a spreadsheet, and someone else follows up by message. Each piece exists, but the full trend is hard to see.
- **Speaker note**: The point is not that people are careless; the workflow itself makes learning difficult.
- **Must keep**: Separate updates hide the pattern.
- **Free to change**: Character mood, number of scattered objects, and visual emphasis.
- **Route guidance**: Route A preferred structure is editable before/after or pain cards with raster characters. Route B can amplify the stress and confusion.

## 02_solution

- **Claim**: One app can connect input, approval, notice, and summary.
- **Support**: The same loop can show who entered the information, who approved it, which notification went out, and what changed in the summary view.
- **Speaker note**: This slide should make the operating loop visible at a glance.
- **Must keep**: Input -> Approval -> Notice -> Summary.
- **Free to change**: Loop shape, helper character, icon metaphors, and scene framing.
- **Route guidance**: Route A should keep the four-step loop as editable PPT objects. Route B can turn it into a full illustrative loop.

## 03_continue

- **Claim**: Improvement continues when every small fix leaves a trace.
- **Support**: A visible history makes it easier to review, reuse, and expand successful changes across teams.
- **Speaker note**: Close by shifting from single issue resolution to repeatable operational learning.
- **Must keep**: Small fixes leave a trace.
- **Free to change**: Closing scene, expressions, celebratory tone, and continuity of recurring people/helper.
- **Route guidance**: Route A should use a clean editable summary and next action. Route B can show the same people with more positive expressions.
